What is Chainmaille?

Chainmaille, also known as maille, chain mail, chainmail, and mail, is the weaving of interconnected rings into a fabric like mesh or chain for use as an armour, jewelry, or decorative art and sculptures.

These days we define “mail” as it was defined in the Middle Ages. It consists of a “fabric” of interlocked metal rings which form a strong, flexible, mesh armour.Â The word “mail” is derived through the Old English mayle, French maille, and Italian maglia, from the Latin macula meaning the mesh of a net. (Arador Armoury, 2013)

Those of us that work in this craft/art, prefer the use of the French "maille" or "chainmaille". This helps to differentiate what we do with those annoying letters promising bad luck if you fail to send it to so many people within so many days or hours.
